Steps to Make Kaju Katli at Home

Kaju Katli is one of the most loved North Indian Sweets. No one can resist it for its unique and delicious taste and subtle sweetness. Kaju is of course cashewnut and Katli means kernel made by thickening milk with sugar and other ingredients (such as dry fruits and mild spices). These katlis are made as thin as 5 mm at the most, and are normally cut into traditional diamond shape.

I have been working as a farmer for about 30 years now and kaju katli isd the most preferred sweet for most of th fesdtivals we celebrate in our village. Once a good friend of mine said "Why make mango burfi again and again? Try and make something different". I agreed and decided to make Mango Kaju Katli. Having a good hand in making mango barfis, making mango kaju katli for the first time was not much difficult for me.

Before making these kaju katlis, others' failures guided me what to do and what not to do. Here are a few tips to make perfect kaju katlis:

1. Making sugar syrup first is mandatory, as kaju katlis become soft but not brittle that way, and they remain fresh for days. Sometimes making one string sugar syrup may get overdone and the katlis may develop sugar crystals which may make them harder. Adding lemon juice or honey is a potential way to prevent it. So, add a tablespoon of any one of these to achieve perfect texture for the sugar syrup. For Mango Kaju katli it is not necessary to add them, as mango pulp takes care of it.

2. Add right amount of sugar to kaju katlis. Too less or more may spoil the taste. According to my experience for 1 cup of cashewnut powder, we can add little over 1/2 cup of sugar. Mixing in little corn flour and sugar in the end makes them smooth but firm.

3. Tiny cashewnut bits are fairly cheaper than whole cashewnuts and grinding them into a powder in the mill will also be quicker.

4. Avoid adding cardamom powder if you add saffron, fruit pulp or rose essence to katlis.

5. Spread the hot mixture from the pan on the kitchen work area. Cover it with a plastic or butter paper sheet and roll with a roller pin as soon as you spread the mixture. That way you will get an even layer of katli which will also be easier to cut into shape of diamonds.

6. While storing the katlis, better keep a layer of butter paper in between each layer of katlis, so that they don't stick to each other.

Follow this recipe and I am sure, you can parallel even the best kaju katlis available in the world!

Ingredients:

  • Cashewnut bits - 1.5 cup, finely powdered
  • Fresh Mango pulp/puree - 1 Cup
  • Sugar - 3/4 Cup
  • Castor Sugar - 4 Tsp
  • Corn Flour - 2 Tbsp
  • Saffron - A pinch soaked in 1 Tbsp hot milk and a pinch for garnishing
  • Ghee - 2 Tsp

Method:

  • Heat a tawa, add one teaspoon desi ghee, sugar and 2 Tbsp water.
  • As the sugar starts to melt and foam, add mango pulp and keep stirring until the syrup thickens and becomes sticky.
  • Add cashewnut powder and keep mixing for 2-3 minutes, and take care not to burn the contents.
  • Add saffron soaked in milk.
  • Keep mixing on medium low heat, until the batter starts to leave the sides.
  • Add corn flour and mix it well for 2 more minutes.
  • Knead the mixture well using your palm, flatten on the greased kitchen work area.
  • Cover it with plastic sheet or butter paper and roll into 5 mm thick spread.
  • Remove the covering sheet, sprinkle saffron and roll again to embed saffron.
  • Cut into equal size diamond shaped burfis with a sharp knife.
  • Once completely cool, loosen with the sharp knife edge and store in an airtight box, preferably layered with butter paper.

      • You can keep them at room temperature for 2-3 days, then store in a cool place.
